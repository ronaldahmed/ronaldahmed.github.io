About me

I just graduated from Mechatronics Engineering (Robotics) at National University of Engineering (UNI) in Lima, Peru. There, I was advised by Alberto Coronado in research projects in Machine Learning.
Currently, I serve as vice-president of the Artificial Intelligence & Control Systems Research Laboratory (GISCIA) in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at UNI.
At GISCIA, we encourage students to do research in Machine Learning, and compete in programming competences. I organize weekly meetings to expose them to the ongoing research in the lab. From time to time, I give seminars and workshops opened to everybody in order to encourage people from other areas and institutions join our research.

Research Interests

I am mainly interested in the application of Machine Learning to NLP tasks and Robotics, specially the symbol grounding problem and language grounding task.

Some interesting trends I have been following lately are deep learning, automatic image captioning, distributed/parallel machine learning, learning representations, robot navigation, among others.

Here is a more detailed list of areas I find interesting.

  • Natural Language Processing: syntactic and semantic parsing, cross-lingual models, statistical machine translation, topic modeling, summarization.
  • Machine learning: probabilistic graphical models, structured prediction, deep learning, learning representations, tensor modeling.
  • Robotics: symbol grounding problem, spacial-semantic representation, SLAM.
  • Language grounding, image and video captioning.
  • Interactive data visualization
